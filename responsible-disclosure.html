Security & Responsible Disclosure Policy

Last Updated: 2026

Our Commitment

Security is a priority for zishanhack.com. We welcome responsible security research conducted in good faith and in accordance with this policy.

Scope

  • https://zishanhack.com
  • Official subdomains directly owned and operated by us

Assets not explicitly listed are considered out of scope.

Out of Scope

  • Denial of Service (DoS/DDoS) attacks
  • Automated scanning that impacts availability
  • Social engineering attacks
  • Third-party services, vendors, or integrations
  • Physical attacks
  • Spam or rate-limit testing

Testing Rules

  • No data exfiltration or copying of sensitive information
  • No modification or deletion of user data
  • No persistence mechanisms, shells, or backdoors
  • Stop testing immediately upon accessing sensitive data
  • Do not impact availability or degrade performance

Safe Harbor

If you comply strictly with this policy and conduct testing in good faith within scope, we will not pursue legal action for security research activities related to your report.

This safe harbor applies only to activities that adhere fully to the rules defined above.

Reporting Process

Submit vulnerability reports to:

Email: report@zishanhack.com

Please include:

  • Vulnerability category
  • Affected URL or endpoint
  • Clear impact explanation
  • Step-by-step reproduction instructions
  • Proof-of-concept (if applicable)

We aim to acknowledge reports within 5 business days.

Compensation

We do not operate a public bug bounty program. Submission of a vulnerability report does not create any entitlement to financial compensation.

Any goodwill gestures, if offered, are made solely at our discretion.

Public Disclosure

Public disclosure is permitted only after written confirmation that the vulnerability has been remediated.

Security Hall of Fame

We recognize researchers who responsibly disclose valid vulnerabilities in compliance with this policy.

No public acknowledgments at this time.

Inclusion in the Hall of Fame is granted at our sole discretion and requires:

  • Valid and non-duplicate vulnerability
  • Full compliance with testing rules
  • No exploitation or abuse of discovered issues
  • No premature or unauthorized disclosure

Submission of a report does not guarantee public recognition.
